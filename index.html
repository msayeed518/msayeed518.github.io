About

Since before I could write, I've loved to create. As a small child, I made little comics without words and eventually became a self-taught artist. I thought I wanted to pursue the art field for a career, but in college realized the core of what I enjoyed about art was creating something out of nothing.

Fast forward to junior year of college, when I was a Marketing and Events intern at Citizens' Climate Lobby. I randomly mentioned a video project I was working on for a digital arts class to my supervisors. They both watched my draft and asked me to fill their video editor role. Of course, I said yes! I love to combine both my creative and technical skills in video editing.

While I was learning a lot in my digital arts minor at Stony Brook University, there was a lot to teach myself in order to create not only artistically meaningful and technically precise videos, but also videos suited for social media. Eventually I created a video for their bi-annual international conference, which was very well received. This lead me to my video editing job at Love What Matters.

At Love What Matters, I faced a new challenge of learning to content curate and direct video participants for a meaningful story on brand with their other content. Through trial and error, I quickly picked up content curation and delivered 2-3 episodes of the Daily Dose of You series per week while working on another series called the Adoptive Truth. I enjoyed applying a variety of my skills here; I was able to communicate with others, curate content, edit videos and construct a narrative in a creative way.

Creation has always been my passion. You will find that I excel in a variety of areas: technical skills, creativity, and communication. I will always deliver a quality, polished final product.
